Notes

All mechanics requires shields! | ➔ Indicates Carry Over

Targeted mitigation must be on your firewalled target unless the firewall is down. For the most part, most targeted mitigation is mostly filler and does not work on raidwides. It is mainly used for minimizing tank autos and/or busters.

Both tanks will get attacked for moderately high damage throughout the entire phase, ensure you are rolling mitigation and heals on them.

Healers will need to manage HP burst accordingly to ensure that Accretions are not popped together. The H1 and H2 can throw single target heals at whoever has the Accretion between them.

If playing AST, ensure the vulnerability has expired before popping Macrocosmos. WHM can use Benediction (if not used earlier) to instantly pop the healer Accretion.

  • ¹At the beginning of the phase, use 30s mitigation after (when the textbox disappears) Kefka says, "Oh! What other toys can I throw in here..." to get tank autos and the raidwide + an additional usage for Stray Flames/Tsunami.
  • ²There is a very small period where you can cover both hits of Thunder III and the next Stray Flames/Tsumani, if you miss the timing, you can use it next GCD.
  • ³Use if holding Chaos, otherwise use at the beginning of P4 for autos.
  • ⁴Non-healers should avoid using any healing abilities that may cause their Accretion to pop early such as Second Wind, Curing Waltz or Divine Veil. If both Accretions are activated in a short amount of time, it will cause a wipe.
  • ⁵If you are holding Exdeath instead of Chaos at the beginning, use Reprisal on both before The Decisive Battle finishes.
  • ⁶Use LB3 at the W of Vacuum Wave. Either tank can press it, discuss beforehand.
  • ⁷Seraphism can be shifted to P4 if you feel you have sufficient mitigation.
  • ⁸Prepare Spreadlo on the tanks, prioritizing WAR > DRK > GNB/PLD.
  • ⁹Prepare immediately after Bowels of Agony.

Tank FAQ

common questions, answered
Why don't you have mitigation for reverse compositions such as DRK/WAR?

For a PF setting, it is too confusing to have a seperate plan just because you want to swap MT/OT. My recommendation is to follow the plan and follow the positions in the strats. For example, if you plan to run MT DRK and OT WAR, you can follow what is on the WAR/DRK page, but when doing mechanics, the DRK will stand in MT positions and the WAR in OT.

Why are there no invulns for some jobs in P2?

P3 has five busters that need to be invulned as well as swapped with mitigation. Due to the mechanic occurring during the start of P3, it is quite tedious to execute a swap and an invuln is highly recommended. Due to the large CDs of Superbolide and Hallowed Ground, we avoid using invuln for specific comps such as PLD/DRK and PLD/GNB. This also allows all comps to follow the same order in P3: Invuln, Share, Share, Invuln and Share.

Why do you keep changing things on the sheet?

While I can understand it's frustrating to do a lockout and see something change, it is also very early days and I need to consider the best possible mitigation that will benefit PF long term. While I feel like most mitigation is pretty much sorted out for now, please continue to check the changelog for any changes. You can join my Discord server (link in the credits page) or @Gardevoir101 in NAUR or MUR and I will respond when I can.

Why is there a large focus on the invulning tank holding Exdeath?

Because we want the tank controlling Chaos for the first Thunder III to not pull Chaos away during the first Thunder III. Additionally, for the fourth Thunder III, we want the tank invulning to have main threat on Exdeath during Black Hole tethers. If the other tank has aggro, they may position Exdeath in a way that makes it diffcult for the invulning tank to reach Exdeath before Thunder III goes off. For the first Thunder III, this is avoided if Exdeath is held middle.
